What is TWICE?

The members of TWICE. From left to right: Jeongyeon, Mina, Jihyo, Dahyun, Momo, Chaeyoung, Sana, Tzuyu, and Nayeon.

TWICE is a female Korean pop group consisting of nine members under JYP Entertainment. They debuted in 2015 and have been together ever since. They have released four studio albums, two compilation albums, four reissues, nine extended plays, one compilation extended play, and twenty seven singles as of now, and are the highest-selling girl group in South Korea.

The music

The debut

On October 20, 2015, TWICE debuted with the extended play The Story Begins, with the title track "Like Ooh-Ahh." Though they had a budding fandom, the EP was far from their most successful tracks.

The rise to fame

On April 25, 2016, TWICE released their second EP, Page Two, with the title track "Cheer Up." This track recieved much more love and support than their debut, and lifted them into the spotlight. From this point on, TWICE was South Korea's "nation's girl group." Later, they'd begin to be referred to as "Asia's girl group."

The latest

TWICE's latest release is their second full length album titled Eyes Wide Open, and showcases a more mature and versatile TWICE, with lyrics written by the members themselves and the title track "I Can't Stop Me." Stuyvesant's own Stuy Squad did a cover of the title during this year's Stuy Squad Showcase.
